Pocket Monsters: Johto

A refined version of the classic GBC adventure.

About the game

Pocket Monsters: Johto is an open source enhancement to the original Pokémon Crystal on the Gameboy Colour. The core design philosophies SKELSOFT stuck to when developing this fangame were replayability, compatibility, and player freedom. The original games difficulty was overhauled with a careful hand, allowing for more challenging moments and enjoyable gameplay while refusing to restrict the player's progression any more than the original game, all while remaining compatible with the original cartridges.

Part of what makes the earlier incarnations of the Pokémon series so charming is its free-form player progression, allowing you to do certain things in almost any order. Explore the world at your own pace. We have tried to recapture that wanderlust of the early adventures. We hope you enjoy revisiting the world of Pokémon, as if it never left the year 2000.

Features

  • Experience the original Johto journey, with its robust post-game adventure given an extra helping!
  • Locations from Red and Blue that were removed have been restored, and some new locations added to open up the overworld. Rediscover the Seafoam Islands, wade through Viridian Forest, or surf over Cherrygrove Bay!
  • New engine mechanics, such as re-use of Repel, unlimited TMs, Running Shoes, and using Rock Smash in overworld sometimes containing items.
  • All 251 Pokémon available for capture, battle, and trading with friends!
  • Original game compatibility! Trade and battle with friends on the original Pokémon Gold, Silver, or Crystal versions!
  • Pokémon Stadium 2 compatible! Finished your two-region adventure? Caught them all? Take your Pokémon champions over to the N64 and become the penultimate Gen II master!

Open Source

Based on the pokecrystal disassembly by pret, this is an open source fangame built on the bones of the original Pokémon Crystal on the Gameboy Colour. That means intrepid digital necromancers with the know-how could peek underneath the curtain to see how our game was made, or even edit it themselves to their liking!
